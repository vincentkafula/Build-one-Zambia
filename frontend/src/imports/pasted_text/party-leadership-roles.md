
PARTY LEADERSHIP
Roles, Responsibilities & Functions

This document outlines the leadership structure of the party across all levels — National, Provincial, District, and Branch. It describes the designation and detailed functions of each official position, providing a clear framework for governance, accountability, and organisational effectiveness.

1. NATIONAL LEADERSHIP
The National Leadership constitutes the highest governing body of the party. These officials are elected at the National Conference and are collectively responsible for the overall strategic direction, policy formulation, and governance of the organisation at a national level.
President
The President is the highest-ranking official of the party and serves as the face and ultimate authority of the organisation. The President provides overall vision, strategic direction, and moral leadership to all structures of the party at every level.
Key Functions:
•  Serve as the chief spokesperson and public representative of the party.
•  Preside over all National Executive Committee (NEC) and General Council meetings.
•  Provide strategic and ideological direction to the party.
•  Represent the party in all official national and international engagements.
•  Appoint and oversee the work of the Secretary General and other senior officials.
•  Ensure the party's constitution, policies, and resolutions are upheld.
•  Serve as the final authority on matters of party discipline at the national level.
•  Report to the National General Council and National Conference on the state of the party.

Deputy President
The Deputy President supports the President in all leadership functions and assumes full presidential responsibilities in the absence or incapacitation of the President. The Deputy President plays a critical role in coordinating party programmes and bridging the leadership with various structures.
Key Functions:
•  Assist the President in executing the strategic mandate of the party.
•  Act as President when the President is absent or unable to perform duties.
•  Coordinate between the national leadership and provincial structures.
•  Champion specific policy portfolios as assigned by the President.
•  Participate in all NEC meetings and contribute to major decisions.
•  Represent the party at events and engagements as delegated by the President.
•  Provide oversight on party programmes and campaign activities.
•  Assist in mediating conflicts within party structures at the national level.

Secretary General
The Secretary General is the chief administrator and organisational backbone of the party. This role ensures the efficient functioning of all party structures and the faithful implementation of resolutions passed by the highest decision-making bodies.
Key Functions:
•  Manage the day-to-day administrative operations of the party's national office.
•  Record and distribute minutes, resolutions, and communiqués from all NEC meetings.
•  Coordinate communication between the national leadership and all lower structures.
•  Maintain the official membership register and organisational records.
•  Ensure compliance with the party's constitution and standing rules at all levels.
•  Oversee the planning and logistics of national conferences and campaigns.
•  Issue official party correspondence and manage internal communications.
•  Serve as custodian of the party's official documents, archives, and seal.

Deputy Secretary General
The Deputy Secretary General supports the Secretary General in all administrative and organisational responsibilities and assumes the Secretary General's role when required. This role is critical in maintaining organisational continuity and effective administration.
Key Functions:
•  Assist the Secretary General in managing party administration and operations.
•  Act as Secretary General in their absence or when delegated.
•  Coordinate specific administrative portfolios as assigned by the Secretary General.
•  Support the preparation and distribution of meeting agendas and documents.
•  Liaise with provincial secretaries to ensure reporting obligations are met.
•  Monitor implementation of NEC resolutions at all party levels.
•  Assist in managing correspondence and official party communications.
•  Support membership verification and organisational capacity-building initiatives.

Chairperson
The National Chairperson is responsible for presiding over all meetings of the party's national structures in an impartial and procedurally sound manner. The Chairperson ensures orderly deliberation and upholds the dignity and rules of engagement in all forums.
Key Functions:
•  Preside over all NEC, National Working Committee, and General Council meetings.
•  Ensure meetings are conducted in accordance with the party constitution and standing orders.
•  Maintain order, decorum, and fairness during all deliberations.
•  Facilitate debate and ensure all members are afforded equal opportunity to speak.
•  Rule on procedural matters and points of order.
•  Guide the adoption and implementation of meeting resolutions.
•  Work closely with the Secretary General to ensure effective meeting management.
•  Support the President in coordinating broader national party engagements.

Deputy Chairperson
The Deputy Chairperson assists the Chairperson and assumes chairperson duties in their absence. This role supports the smooth facilitation of meetings and contributes to the broader governance responsibilities of the national leadership collective.
Key Functions:
•  Act as Chairperson when the Chairperson is absent or unavailable.
•  Assist in the preparation and management of meeting agendas.
•  Support the Chairperson in enforcing meeting rules and procedures.
•  Facilitate sub-committee meetings and special working group sessions.
•  Assist in mentoring and capacitating lower-level chairpersons in the party.
•  Contribute to governance reviews and constitutional matters.
•  Represent the Chairperson at events and meetings as delegated.
•  Promote participation and inclusive deliberation within party structures.

Treasurer General
The Treasurer General is the chief financial officer of the party and bears ultimate accountability for the financial health, integrity, and sustainability of the organisation. This role ensures that all financial matters are managed transparently and in accordance with applicable laws and party policy.
Key Functions:
•  Oversee the financial management of all national party funds and accounts.
•  Present annual budgets and financial reports to the NEC and General Council.
•  Ensure all income and expenditure is properly recorded and accounted for.
•  Supervise fundraising, donor relations, and financial sustainability strategies.
•  Ensure compliance with financial regulations, tax laws, and auditing requirements.
•  Authorise national-level expenditure in line with approved budgets.
•  Maintain oversight of financial controls and prevent financial misconduct.
•  Coordinate financial reporting from provincial and lower structures.

Deputy Treasurer General
The Deputy Treasurer General supports the Treasurer General in all financial management duties and assumes full financial responsibilities in the Treasurer General's absence. This role is vital in ensuring financial continuity and accountability at the national level.
Key Functions:
•  Assist the Treasurer General in managing the party's national finances.
•  Act as Treasurer General when the Treasurer General is absent or unavailable.
•  Support the preparation and presentation of financial statements and budgets.
•  Assist in managing donor relationships and fundraising activities.
•  Monitor financial compliance across provincial and lower structures.
•  Support internal and external auditing processes.
•  Contribute to the development of financial policies and controls.
•  Liaise with provincial treasurers to ensure financial reporting is complete and timely.


2. PROVINCIAL LEADERSHIP
The Provincial Leadership is responsible for driving the party's mandate within a specific province. These officials are elected at the Provincial Conference and work to implement national resolutions while responding to the unique political and social dynamics of their province.
Chairperson
The Provincial Chairperson leads the party's provincial executive and represents the party's authority within the province. This role ensures provincial activities are aligned with national policy and that the party's presence is effectively built across the province.
Key Functions:
•  Lead and preside over the Provincial Executive Committee (PEC) meetings.
•  Represent the party in all provincial political, government, and civic engagements.
•  Ensure provincial programmes align with national strategies and resolutions.
•  Coordinate provincial campaigns, elections, and mobilisation activities.
•  Report to the national leadership on the state of the province.
•  Provide political oversight on governance matters in the province.
•  Resolve disputes and disciplinary matters escalated from lower structures.
•  Champion the party's values and vision within the province.

Deputy Chairperson
The Provincial Deputy Chairperson supports the Chairperson and assumes leadership of the province in the Chairperson's absence. This role is integral to building provincial capacity and maintaining effective coordination across districts.
Key Functions:
•  Assist the Chairperson in leading provincial structures and engagements.
•  Act as Chairperson when the Chairperson is absent or incapacitated.
•  Oversee specific portfolios and programmes as assigned by the Chairperson.
•  Support liaison between the PEC and district leadership structures.
•  Facilitate provincial workshops, training, and capacity-building programmes.
•  Contribute to the development of provincial strategies and action plans.
•  Assist in conflict resolution and mediation within provincial structures.
•  Represent the province at national engagements as delegated.

Secretary
The Provincial Secretary is the chief administrator of the province and manages the operational and organisational functions of the provincial party office. This role is the key link between the national secretariat and all district structures.
Key Functions:
•  Manage the administrative operations of the provincial party office.
•  Record, distribute, and archive minutes and resolutions of PEC meetings.
•  Coordinate communication between the PEC and district secretaries.
•  Maintain provincial membership records and organisational databases.
•  Ensure resolutions from the PEC and national structures are implemented.
•  Oversee the planning of provincial conferences, campaigns, and events.
•  Issue official provincial correspondence and manage communications.
•  Report regularly to the Secretary General on provincial organisational matters.

Deputy Secretary
The Provincial Deputy Secretary supports the Secretary in all administrative functions and acts as Secretary when required. This role contributes to building organisational efficiency and ensuring effective communication across all levels of the province.
Key Functions:
•  Assist the Provincial Secretary in managing provincial administration.
•  Act as Secretary in the Secretary's absence.
•  Support the drafting and distribution of meeting agendas and reports.
•  Liaise with district secretaries to monitor reporting and compliance.
•  Assist in the implementation of resolutions from PEC and national structures.
•  Support the coordination of provincial events, campaigns, and activities.
•  Maintain provincial filing systems and documentation standards.
•  Contribute to membership recruitment and database management.

Treasurer General
The Provincial Treasurer General manages the financial affairs of the provincial party structure and ensures accountability, transparency, and sound financial management throughout the province.
Key Functions:
•  Manage all provincial party funds, accounts, and financial transactions.
•  Prepare and present provincial budgets and financial reports to the PEC.
•  Ensure all income and expenditure is properly recorded and audited.
•  Oversee provincial fundraising and resource mobilisation activities.
•  Ensure compliance with financial policies set by the national Treasurer General.
•  Authorise provincial expenditure within approved budgets.
•  Provide financial oversight on district-level financial activities.
•  Report to the National Treasurer General on provincial financial matters.

Deputy Treasurer
The Provincial Deputy Treasurer supports the Treasurer General and assumes financial responsibilities in the Treasurer's absence. This role helps ensure financial integrity and continuity at the provincial level.
Key Functions:
•  Assist the Provincial Treasurer General in managing provincial finances.
•  Act as Treasurer when the Treasurer General is absent.
•  Support the preparation of provincial financial statements and budgets.
•  Monitor financial activities of district structures and ensure compliance.
•  Assist in coordinating provincial fundraising and resource mobilisation.
•  Support auditing and financial review processes.
•  Help develop and enforce financial controls within the province.
•  Report financial progress to the PEC as required.


3. DISTRICT LEADERSHIP
The District Leadership coordinates party activities across branches within a defined district. These officials bridge the gap between provincial leadership and branch structures, ensuring effective implementation of programmes and growth of the party at the local government level.
Chairperson
The District Chairperson leads the party's district executive and is responsible for building the party's strength and influence at the district level. This role coordinates all political and organisational activities across branches within the district.
Key Functions:
•  Preside over all District Executive Committee (DEC) meetings.
•  Represent the party in district-level political, civic, and government forums.
•  Ensure district activities align with provincial and national mandates.
•  Coordinate campaigns, elections, and community mobilisation in the district.
•  Report to the provincial leadership on the state of the district.
•  Oversee the development and support of branches within the district.
•  Resolve escalated disputes from branch structures.
•  Champion the party's values and programmes throughout the district.

Deputy Chairperson
The District Deputy Chairperson supports the Chairperson and assumes leadership when required. This role assists in building the organisational capacity of district and branch structures.
Key Functions:
•  Assist the District Chairperson in leading the DEC.
•  Act as Chairperson in the Chairperson's absence.
•  Oversee specific programmes and portfolios as assigned by the Chairperson.
•  Support coordination between the DEC and branch leadership.
•  Facilitate capacity-building and training for branch officials.
•  Contribute to district planning and programme development.
•  Assist in mediating conflicts within district structures.
•  Represent the district at provincial meetings as delegated.

Secretary
The District Secretary manages the administrative and organisational functions of the district party structure. This role is the primary link between the provincial secretariat and all branch secretaries within the district.
Key Functions:
•  Manage the administrative operations of the district party structure.
•  Record and distribute minutes and resolutions of DEC meetings.
•  Coordinate communication between the DEC and branch secretaries.
•  Maintain district membership records and organisational data.
•  Ensure implementation of resolutions from the DEC and provincial structures.
•  Plan and coordinate district conferences, campaigns, and events.
•  Issue official district correspondence and manage communications.
•  Report regularly to the Provincial Secretary on district matters.

Deputy Secretary
The District Deputy Secretary supports the Secretary and acts in the Secretary's capacity when required. This role is essential to the smooth administrative functioning of the district.
Key Functions:
•  Assist the District Secretary in all administrative duties.
•  Act as Secretary in the Secretary's absence.
•  Support preparation and distribution of meeting agendas and documents.
•  Liaise with branch secretaries to ensure reporting requirements are met.
•  Monitor the implementation of district and provincial resolutions.
•  Support the coordination of district events and campaigns.
•  Maintain accurate district records and documentation.
•  Contribute to membership growth and database management.

Treasurer
The District Treasurer manages the financial affairs of the district party structure and ensures that all financial activities are conducted with transparency, accountability, and compliance with party financial policy.
Key Functions:
•  Manage all district party funds and financial transactions.
•  Prepare and present district budgets and financial reports to the DEC.
•  Ensure all district income and expenditure is properly recorded.
•  Oversee district fundraising and resource mobilisation efforts.
•  Ensure compliance with provincial and national financial guidelines.
•  Authorise district-level expenditure within approved budgets.
•  Provide oversight on branch financial activities.
•  Report to the Provincial Treasurer on district financial performance.

Deputy Treasurer
The District Deputy Treasurer supports the Treasurer and maintains financial operations in the Treasurer's absence. This role promotes sound financial management across the district.
Key Functions:
•  Assist the District Treasurer in managing district finances.
•  Act as Treasurer when the Treasurer is absent or unavailable.
•  Support preparation of district financial reports and budgets.
•  Monitor branch-level financial activities and ensure compliance.
•  Assist in district fundraising and resource mobilisation activities.
•  Contribute to financial reviews and audit processes.
•  Help enforce financial controls and prevent mismanagement.
•  Report financial updates to the DEC as required.


4. BRANCH LEADERSHIP
The Branch Leadership is the most grassroots tier of the party. Branch officials are elected at Branch General Meetings and are the direct interface between the party and the community. Their work is vital in building membership, delivering programmes, and representing the party at the local level.
Chairperson
The Branch Chairperson is the most grassroots leader in the party structure and serves as the primary point of contact between the party and the community. This role is critical in building the party's base, recruiting members, and implementing party programmes at the community level.
Key Functions:
•  Preside over all Branch General Meetings (BGM) and Branch Executive Committee (BEC) meetings.
•  Represent the party in the local community and at district-level engagements.
•  Ensure branch activities align with district, provincial, and national directives.
•  Lead local campaigns, voter registration drives, and community mobilisation.
•  Report to district leadership on the state of the branch.
•  Oversee the growth and development of branch membership.
•  Resolve member disputes and enforce party discipline at branch level.
•  Promote the party's vision, values, and policies within the community.

Deputy Chairperson
The Branch Deputy Chairperson supports the Chairperson in leading the branch and assumes full chairperson responsibilities when required. This role helps grow organisational capacity and ensures the branch operates effectively.
Key Functions:
•  Assist the Branch Chairperson in leading the BEC.
•  Act as Chairperson when the Chairperson is absent.
•  Oversee specific portfolios and local programmes as assigned.
•  Support member recruitment, retention, and engagement.
•  Assist in facilitation of branch meetings and community activities.
•  Coordinate sub-structures or ward activities within the branch.
•  Contribute to branch planning and action plans.
•  Represent the branch at district meetings as delegated.

Secretary
The Branch Secretary is responsible for the administrative functioning of the branch. This role ensures all meetings are properly documented, members are registered, and the branch maintains effective communication with higher structures.
Key Functions:
•  Manage the day-to-day administrative duties of the branch.
•  Record and circulate minutes and resolutions of all BEC and BGM meetings.
•  Maintain the branch membership register and attendance records.
•  Coordinate communication between the BEC and the district secretary.
•  Ensure implementation of resolutions from the BEC and higher structures.
•  Plan and prepare agendas for branch meetings and events.
•  Issue official branch correspondence and manage internal communications.
•  Report to the district secretary on branch organisational matters.

Deputy Secretary
The Branch Deputy Secretary supports the Secretary in managing branch administration and acts as Secretary when required. This role is important in building the administrative capacity of the branch.
Key Functions:
•  Assist the Branch Secretary in all administrative and clerical duties.
•  Act as Secretary in the Secretary's absence.
•  Help prepare meeting agendas and documentation.
•  Assist in maintaining membership records and branch registers.
•  Support the coordination of branch activities and campaigns.
•  Assist with correspondence and communication management.
•  Ensure minutes are properly recorded and distributed to members.
•  Contribute to the organisation and filing of branch documents.

Treasurer
The Branch Treasurer manages the financial affairs of the branch and ensures that all financial activities are conducted with transparency, honesty, and accountability. This role is the financial steward of the most grassroots level of the party.
Key Functions:
•  Manage all branch funds, collections, and financial transactions.
•  Prepare and present branch financial statements at BEC and BGM meetings.
•  Collect and account for membership subscriptions, levies, and contributions.
•  Ensure all income and expenditure is properly recorded with supporting documents.
•  Oversee local fundraising activities and ensure funds are banked promptly.
•  Comply with district and provincial financial guidelines and policies.
•  Authorise branch expenditure within approved budgets.
•  Report to the district treasurer on branch financial activities.

Deputy Treasurer
The Branch Deputy Treasurer supports the Treasurer in managing branch finances and assumes treasury responsibilities in the Treasurer's absence. This role promotes financial accountability at the branch level.
Key Functions:
•  Assist the Branch Treasurer in managing all financial activities.
•  Act as Treasurer when the Treasurer is absent or unavailable.
•  Support the collection and recording of membership subscriptions and funds.
•  Assist in preparing financial reports for BEC and BGM meetings.
•  Help oversee branch fundraising activities and resource management.
•  Ensure financial records are kept accurately and up to date.
•  Assist in auditing and reviewing branch financial activities.
•  Report on financial matters to the BEC and district treasurer as required.
